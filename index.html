In the News

Muse was conceived of in response to the AMA Medical Education Innovation Challenge—a nationwide call to engage medical students in improving the educational system around them. Among an outstanding pool of 146 proposals from peers around the United States, we are honored that Muse was selected as the Innovation Challenge winner.

Meet Our Team

Amol Utrankar

Second year M.D. Candidate, Vanderbilt University

Amol is interested in health systems design, technology, and medical education. Originally from Edison, New Jersey, Amol graduated from Rice University in 2014 with a B.A. in Economics. At Vanderbilt, Amol is a member of the Student Technology Committee, whose goal is to implement and improve technologies to support undergraduate medical education. He has also served as a content developer for an EdX massive open online course, “Medicine in the Digital Age," and is an advisor to Stanford University's Medicine X conference and the First Aid/USMLE Rx learning platform. In the future, he intends to pursue a career in anesthesiology and clinical informatics.

Jared Shenson

Fourth year M.D. Candidate, Vanderbilt University

Jared's interests span design thinking, biomedical informatics, and health technology and their intersection with surgical care and medical education. He originally hails from the San Francisco Bay Area and graduated from Yale University with a B.S. in Molecular and Cellular Biology. During his tenure at Vanderbilt, Jared founded and lead the Student Technology Committee, helped to manage a curricular transition as a member of Curriculum Committee, and was actively engaged in research in medical education and clinical informatics. Previously, he has consulted for multiple consumer and educational technology companies. He will be graduating in May and pursuing a career in otolaryngology and clinical informatics.
